What is it

Cool Tools for School, is a big OneNote notebook with a lot of tools for student.

It contains a set of well-organized, structured and linked pages and templates, tips for organization, study, note-taking and research, and many resources and tools such as stickers, insertable mini-templates, and posters with educational information for all levels.

All this information is organized in such a way that it can be used as a "system" and not as simple templates.

What is it for?

With its templates, tools and resources, Cool Tools for School helps the student with the following:

  • Manage classes, assignments and exams,
  • Organize their time,
  • Plan study days,
  • Prepare for exams,
  • Set goals,
  • Measure progress,
  • Take notes in class and manage them,
  • And over all..., to generate the turn-in content in a more productive, professional, organized, fun way and with a touch of personal design, inspired by the sample data of each template and design tips.

How it is built

The power of OneNote (with its cloud storage infrastructure, to share your content, page history, backup, etc.) + well-organized templates + guides, documentation and contextual help + insertable mini-templates with standardized design... and a good idea, allows us to develop, what I call "Light Systems". Cool Tools for School is a "Light system" made with OneNote.

Cool Tools for School is developed with 100% of OneNote components (text, tables, links and images) and follows the design and color palette of Microsoft Teams.

My experience as a software engineer allowed me to see it, think about it and design it as a system and not as a simple digital planner.

Market analysis (of digital planners)

There is a very large market for digital planners, and one of the most searched and sold categories is Student Planners.

The current student planners are mostly all the same and are based on a calendar digital planner with added student oriented templates. These are sold through portals like Etsy.com.
Most of those planners are .pdf linked kind and are designed by graphic designers to be used with programs such as Notability or Goodnotes (paid apps).

There are some planners for OneNote, but they are bad adaptations of these .pdfs (pdf as background image) and are not specifically designed for OneNote. There are also a few designed for OneNote but they are a set of basic templates.

Cool Tools for School is not a calendar adapted for students. Cool Tools for School is a true study planner and will be the most complete that exists in the market, specifically thought for students of all levels and designed specifically for OneNote.

Integration with
Microsoft Teams

Of course we know that Microsoft Teams exists to manage everything related to classes, assignments, exams and communication between students and teachers, and it also has a strong integration with OneNote.
But with Cool Tools for School, the students will be able to extend and customize all the information of Microsoft Teams as well as help them to be inspired and create cool content for their turn-ins, notes and research.

Cool Tools for School could be integrated as a separate notebook or as selectable sections when the teacher creates the class and configures the structure of the OneNote class notebook.

(concept)

Ease of use and help

Cool Tools for School could is very easy to use since each page or template has its on-screen help, contextual FAQ list, and usage and design tips.

In addition, each template come with sample data from a real case based on an IT class (not Lorem ipsum) which allows students to see its use and also helps as inspiration to make the most of its potential.

These on-screen help, contextual FAQs, tips and sample data will not only help the user, but will also turn him into a pro OneNote user!
